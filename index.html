FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

An NFT (Non-Fungible Token) is a type of digital asset that represents ownership or proof of authenticity of a unique item using blockchain technology. Unlike cryptocurrencies such as Bitcoin, which are fungible (interchangeable), NFTs are unique and cannot be exchanged on a one-to-one basis. They are often used for digital art, collectibles, music, videos, and other forms of creative work.

NFTs are built on blockchain platforms like Ethereum, which store information about the NFT’s ownership and transfer history. When someone purchases an NFT, they are effectively buying a unique digital certificate linked to a specific item.

NFTs gain value due to their rarity, the artist or creator behind them, and the demand from collectors. Since each NFT is unique or part of a limited series, they can become highly sought after, especially if tied to popular digital art, music, or entertainment figures.

To buy an NFT, you need a cryptocurrency wallet and some cryptocurrency (usually Ethereum) to make the purchase. After setting up a wallet, you can visit an NFT marketplace such as OpenSea, Rarible, or Foundation, where you can browse NFTs for sale. Once you find an NFT you want to purchase, you can connect your wallet to the platform, complete the transaction, and the NFT will be transferred to your wallet.

While you can copy or download the digital file associated with an NFT (such as an image, video, or audio), this does not mean you own the NFT itself.
